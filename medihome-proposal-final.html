1. Introduction

Medicines are part of daily life in many households, especially when family members need regular treatment, vitamins, pain relief, or medicine for chronic conditions. However, in most homes, medicines are stored in drawers, shelves, bags, or cabinets without any organized tracking system. As time passes, people may forget which medicines are available, how much is left, whether they are expired, or when they should be taken. This creates inconvenience and, in some cases, risk.

The proposed project, MediHome, is a home medicine management system designed to help families keep their medicine storage under control. The system focuses on practical household needs such as medicine reminders, expiry-date monitoring, low-stock alerts, and simple medicine inventory tracking. The main purpose of the project is to provide a structured and easy-to-use software solution that improves medicine organization and reduces common mistakes at home.

2. Problem Statement

Many families do not have a reliable way to manage the medicines they keep at home. As a result, they may forget dosage times, keep medicines after they expire, buy duplicates, or suddenly discover that an important medicine is out of stock. These problems often happen because medicine information is not stored in one clear place and is instead managed through memory, paper notes, or manual checking.

The problem is not only about storing medicine, but also about keeping track of essential details such as quantity, expiry date, reminder times, and medicine use for different household members. Therefore, there is a clear need for a simple software system that helps users manage home medicines in a safer and more organized way.

3. Background Survey

Several digital solutions already exist in the area of medicine management, but most of them address only part of the problem. Some mobile applications mainly focus on reminders for a single user, which is useful but limited. Other systems are built for pharmacies or hospitals, where the design is too complex for everyday home use. In many cases, people still depend on handwritten notes or manual inspection of medicine boxes, which does not provide alerts, stock awareness, or shared household visibility.

MediHome is different because it is designed specifically for the home environment. It combines several useful functions in one system: medicine inventory, reminder scheduling, expiry warnings, and low-stock tracking. Instead of acting as a clinical or pharmacy platform, the project focuses on simple household medicine control. This makes the solution more practical, more understandable for regular users, and better aligned with the real problem that families face.

4. Proposed Approach

The proposed solution is to design a user-friendly application that allows users to register and monitor the medicines available at home. Each medicine entry can store details such as medicine name, category, dosage instructions, quantity, expiry date, and reminder schedule. The system will then use this data to support users through clear monitoring and timely alerts.

The expected core features of MediHome include:

  • adding and updating medicines stored at home,
  • setting reminders for medicines that must be taken on schedule,
  • tracking current stock levels,
  • warning users when a medicine is close to expiry or already expired,
  • notifying users when stock becomes low,
  • allowing household-level organization of medicine information.

The project is intended to improve awareness and organization rather than replace professional medical advice. For that reason, MediHome is positioned as a home management solution, not a diagnosis or treatment system.

5. Selected Software Process Model

The most suitable software process model for this project is Agile. MediHome contains several features that can be developed and improved step by step, such as medicine registration, reminders, stock alerts, and expiry monitoring. Agile is appropriate because it supports incremental development and allows the team to refine requirements after receiving feedback during the project.

Agile is also a practical choice for a student group project. The work can be divided into short iterations, and each team member can focus on a specific area such as requirements, interface design, UML modelling, reminder logic, or testing. This makes planning more flexible and supports continuous progress throughout the semester.
